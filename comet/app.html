All About Comets

By:Manomay,Kuhan,Aaruran

A comet is a small icy object that travels through space. When it gets close to the Sun, it heats up and forms a glowing tail. Comets are made of dust, rock, and frozen gases, often called "dirty snowballs."

  • A comet is a small celestial body composed of ice, dust, and rocky particles.
  • Often described as "dirty snowballs," comets orbit the Sun in elongated paths.

ðŸ§Š Composition of a Comet

  • Nucleus: The solid core made up of ice and rock.
  • Coma: A glowing cloud of gas and dust that forms when the comet nears the Sun.
  • Tail: Streams of gas and dust that extend from the comet, always pointing away from the Sun due to solar wind.

ðŸŒŒ Origin and Orbit

  • Comets originate from the Kuiper Belt and the distant Oort Cloud.
  • Their orbits can take them close to the Sun and then back to the outer reaches of the solar system.

ðŸŒŸ Notable Comets

  • Halley's Comet: Visible from Earth approximately every 76 years.
  • Comet NEOWISE: Made a spectacular appearance in 2020.

ðŸ”­ Observing Comets

  • Best viewed with telescopes or binoculars, especially when they are near the Sun.
  • Some comets become bright enough to be seen with the naked eye.

ðŸ§ª Make Your Own Comet (Activity)

Materials Needed:

  • Dry Ice, Soya Sauce, Ammonia, Zip Lock Bags, Mud (Garden Soil), Water
  • Support: Dry Ice Box, Hammer, Plastic Scoop, Towel, Orange Gloves, Newspapers, Measuring Cylinder (10ml), Small Containers

Pre-preparation:

  • Crush dry ice using a towel and hammer (not by students).
  • Store crushed dry ice in dry ice box.
  • Arrange banners and display posters.
  • Wear powder-coated gloves for safety.
  • Strain pebbles from the garden soil.

Activity Steps:

  1. Open a Zip Lock bag and add garden soil.
  2. Add water to make a slurry.
  3. Prepare 10 ml of ammonia and soya sauce in containers.
  4. Add ammonia to the slurry.
  5. Add soya sauce and mix well.
  6. Form a neck in the zip lock bag, leave top open for dry ice.
  7. Add dry ice powder carefully while holding the neck.
  8. Release grip slightly to let the dry ice mix with slurry.
  9. Shape the bag into a ball and hold for 60 seconds.
  10. Let the comet ball freeze fully.

Safety Warning: Never touch dry ice with bare hands. Always wear thick gloves!
